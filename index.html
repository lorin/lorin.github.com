about me

I work as a computer scientist at ISI (Arlington Campus) in the Adaptive Parallel EXecution (APEX) Computing Group.

I study software developers to understand what problems they face and to evaluate whether proposed technologies actually make their lives easier. My current research focuses on increasing the productivity of computational scientists and engineers by making it easier for them to use large-scale computational resources for scientific and engineering applications, including cloud computing resources.

I also have a more official-looking home page.

bio

I received a PhD in computer science from the University of Maryland (2006), an M.S. in electrical engineering from Boston University (2002), and a B.Eng. in computer engineering from McGill University (1999). I was an assistant professor at University of Nebraska-Lincoln from 2006-2008.
